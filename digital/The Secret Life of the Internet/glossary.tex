\namedchapter{Glossary}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Glossary}

\begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced,breakable,colback=SoftPurple!5,colframe=SoftPurple!60,arc=6pt,boxrule=1pt,
  left=10pt,right=10pt,top=10pt,bottom=10pt,title={\faBook\quad All the Words You Learned},

\begin{description}[labelwidth=4cm, leftmargin=4.2cm, style=nextline, itemsep=0.45\baselineskip]

\item[\glossterm{Amplifier}]
A device along an undersea cable that boosts the light signal so it does not fade over distance.
\emph{(Chapter 5)}

\item[\glossterm{Backup}]
A copy of data stored safely somewhere else in case the original is lost.
\emph{(Chapter 8)}

\item[\glossterm{Browser}]
A programme (like Chrome or Safari) that lets you visit websites.
\emph{(Chapter 2)}

\item[\glossterm{Cable-laying ship}]
A ship designed to carry and place undersea cables across the ocean.
\emph{(Chapter 5)}

\item[\glossterm{Cache}]
A nearby copy of data stored to help deliver it faster.
\emph{(Chapter 4)}

\item[\glossterm{Cloud storage}]
Saving files on remote servers so they can be reached from any device.
\emph{(Chapter 8)}

\item[\glossterm{Crawler}]
A programme that visits web pages automatically to gather information for the search engine's index.
\emph{(Chapter 7)}

\item[\glossterm{Data centre}]
A large building full of servers, kept cool, powered, and secure.
\emph{(Chapter 4)}

\item[\glossterm{Device}]
Any gadget that can connect to the internet — phone, tablet, laptop.
\emph{(Chapter 1)}

\item[\glossterm{DNS}]
Domain Name System — a system that turns website names into number addresses.
\emph{(Chapter 2)}

\item[\glossterm{Ethernet}]
A cable that connects a device directly to a router.
\emph{(Chapter 3)}

\item[\glossterm{Header}]
The label on a packet that says where it came from, where it is going, and its sequence number.
\emph{(Chapter 6)}

\item[\glossterm{HTML}]
HyperText Markup Language — the language used to build web pages.
\emph{(Chapter 2)}

\item[\glossterm{Index}]
The huge organised list of words and pages that a search engine builds.
\emph{(Chapter 7)}

\item[\glossterm{Internet}]
A huge worldwide network that connects billions of devices.
\emph{(Chapter 1)}

\item[\glossterm{IP}]
Internet Protocol — the addressing system of the internet.
\emph{(Chapter 6)}

\item[\glossterm{IP address}]
A number that identifies a device or server on the internet.
\emph{(Chapter 2)}

\item[\glossterm{ISP}]
Internet Service Provider — the company that connects your home to the internet.
\emph{(Chapter 1)}

\item[\glossterm{Local network}]
The private network inside your home that connects your devices together.
\emph{(Chapter 3)}

\item[\glossterm{Network}]
A group of devices linked together so they can share information.
\emph{(Chapter 1)}

\item[\glossterm{Optical fibre}]
A thin glass thread that carries data as pulses of light.
\emph{(Chapter 5)}

\item[\glossterm{Packet}]
A small chunk of data sent across the internet, with a numbered label and address.
\emph{(Chapters 1, 6)}

\item[\glossterm{Password}]
A secret code that protects an online account.
\emph{(Chapter 9)}

\item[\glossterm{Privacy}]
Keeping personal information to yourself and choosing who sees it.
\emph{(Chapter 9)}

\item[\glossterm{Query}]
The words or question you type into a search box.
\emph{(Chapter 7)}

\item[\glossterm{Radio waves}]
Invisible energy waves that carry Wi-Fi signals through the air.
\emph{(Chapter 3)}

\item[\glossterm{Ranking}]
The process of sorting search results to show the most useful ones first.
\emph{(Chapter 7)}

\item[\glossterm{Remote server}]
A server that is in a different physical location from the user.
\emph{(Chapter 8)}

\item[\glossterm{Request}]
A message from your browser asking a server for information.
\emph{(Chapter 4)}

\item[\glossterm{Response}]
The answer from the server, containing the web page or data you asked for.
\emph{(Chapter 4)}

\item[\glossterm{Router}]
A device that connects your home network to the internet and directs traffic.
\emph{(Chapters 3, 6)}

\item[\glossterm{Search engine}]
A service that helps you find information on the internet by searching an index.
\emph{(Chapter 7)}

\item[\glossterm{Server}]
A powerful computer that stores information and sends it to people who ask.
\emph{(Chapter 4)}

\item[\glossterm{Streaming}]
Receiving and playing data (like a video) as it arrives, rather than waiting for it all first.
\emph{(Chapters 6, 8)}

\item[\glossterm{TCP}]
Transmission Control Protocol — the system that makes sure all packets arrive correctly.
\emph{(Chapter 6)}

\item[\glossterm{The cloud}]
Servers accessed via the internet, used to store data or run programmes remotely.
\emph{(Chapter 8)}

\item[\glossterm{Undersea cable}]
A cable on the ocean floor that carries internet data between continents.
\emph{(Chapter 5)}

\item[\glossterm{URL}]
Uniform Resource Locator — a web address; the unique location of a website.
\emph{(Chapter 2)}

\item[\glossterm{Website}]
A collection of pages on the internet, stored on a server.
\emph{(Chapter 2)}

\item[\glossterm{Wi-Fi}]
Wireless technology that uses radio waves to connect devices to a network.
\emph{(Chapter 3)}

\end{description}
\end{tcolorbox}
